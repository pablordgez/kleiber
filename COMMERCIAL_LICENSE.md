# Commercial Licensing

This document is a plain-English guide to the repository license. The authoritative legal
terms are in [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE).

## Default model

Kleiber is source-available under `BUSL-1.1`.

You can use, modify, and deploy it without a separate commercial agreement for:

- personal projects
- internal business operations
- consulting and agency work
- client delivery where you are using Kleiber to do the work, not selling Kleiber itself
- education, research, and evaluation
- integrating Kleiber into your own internal tooling or workflows

You need a separate commercial license if you want to monetize Kleiber itself or a
Kleiber-based offering.

## Commercial uses that require a separate license

A paid commercial license is required if you want to:

- sell licenses to Kleiber or a derivative of Kleiber
- offer Kleiber as a hosted, managed, or SaaS product
- white-label, OEM, or resell Kleiber
- bundle Kleiber into a paid commercial product where Kleiber is a material part of the
  paid offering
- sell access to a product or service whose primary value is substantially the same
  functionality provided by Kleiber

## Simple rule of thumb

Making money with Kleiber is allowed.

Examples:

- using Kleiber inside a consultancy, agency, or internal engineering team
- using Kleiber while building software for clients
- using Kleiber as part of your own internal delivery pipeline

Making money off Kleiber requires a commercial license.

Examples:

- charging customers for access to Kleiber itself
- running a paid hosted Kleiber service
- shipping Kleiber as part of a paid product where Kleiber is a key customer-facing
  feature
- selling a close commercial substitute built directly from Kleiber
